About


I'm a Web Developer with art background and tech experience.

I studied Animation & VFX at Academy of Art University in San Francisco. After graduation, I moved to Los Angeles to work on a stop-motion animation film called "Anomalisa" which was nominated for the best animated feature at the Academy Awards in 2016.

After coming back to San Francisco, I joined at Google Play Movies & TV team to work on content management. While I was working as a team lead, I had many opportunities to meet and work with engineers and that experience inspired me to learn coding and computer science.

I completed a 12 week immersive Web Development program at General Assembly. During the program, I built fully functional applications utilizing Ruby, Rails, JavaScript/jQuery, HTML, and CSS with working knowledge in many other languages, frameworks and web development processes.

I'm grateful for all my adventures and experiences that led me to become a creative web developer. I have been enjoying coding and building useful and beautiful web applications. I'd like to continue learning new technologies and staying creative!

Projects

Welcome Wisdom

Welcome Wisdom is a web application that offers volunteering opportunities to individuals who care for senior wellness, and connects volunteers with seniors in need of company. I built this app using Ruby on Rails, PostgreSQL, Materialize, bcrypt, Action Mailer, and Paperclip.

Vagabond

Vagabond is a web application that users can post reviews on cities they've visited, and also comment on other people's reviews. This was a group project, we used Ruby on Rails, PostgreSQL, Bootstrap, and bcrypt. I contributed mainly on back-end development.

Mapp

Mapp is a web application that users can share their favorite locations around the world. This was a group project, we used Ruby on Rails, PostgreSQL, Materialize, bycrypt and Google APIs. I contributed towards mainly front-end development and building routes to display data.
